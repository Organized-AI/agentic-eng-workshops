01

When local matters

The default is the data center. That's fine, until it isn't.

START HONEST

The data center is the right default

One call, no GPUs, prototype in minutes. For most projects that's correct. Don't build a home lab to dodge a $12 bill.

THE REAL QUESTION

It isn't local vs. cloud. It's: when does owning your stack, and keeping the data path private, actually start to pay off?

THE UNCOMFORTABLE PART

You don't care about local yet

01

The small models are narrow

Below ~128GB of memory you get a model that's good at one job, not a generalist. Useful, general-purpose local starts around 128GB+. So it hasn't felt worth the trouble.

02

Your tokens are subsidized

The frontier labs price their plans to win the market, not to earn margin. You get a huge quota for very little, so cost never bites. Why would you leave their data center?

NOTE

Both of those are temporary. Privacy pressure is not.

HEDGE

WHEN LOCAL STARTS TO MATTER

Five triggers

01
Cost at volume
Data-center bills scale with usage. Local is a fixed cost after the hardware.
02
Data you can't send out
Client PII, health, financial, compliance. The 255K-contact problem. Privacy is the product.
03
Latency & reliability
No rate limits, no vendor outages, works offline. Your private API is always up when you are.
04
Control
No silent model swaps, no deprecations. Fine-tune on your own data, on your metal.
05
Independence
You don't bet the company on one vendor's pricing, terms, or data-retention policy.
SAY IT OUT LOUD

The honest tradeoffs

Capability gap

The best frontier models still beat what you run at home on the hardest tasks.

Ops burden

You now run infrastructure: updates, uptime, drivers, quantization.

Upfront cost

Real money on the table before the first token.

THE FRAMING THAT KEEPS YOU HONEST

Local doesn't replace the data center. It takes the workloads where ownership and privacy beat rented capability.

THE HEURISTIC

Use the data center by default. Go local when the work is high-volume, privacy-bound, or latency-sensitive, and doesn't need frontier-tier reasoning.

Shorter: frontier for the hard 10%. Private API for the recurring, sensitive 90%.

PROOF · HOW I ROUTE IT AT WAYFINDER

A consumer GPU (a 24GB card), or Apple Silicon with large unified memory, more headroom per dollar once models get big.

LAYER 02 · RUNTIME

Pick by ease vs. throughput

EASIEST

Ollama / LM Studio

One machine, one user, running in minutes. This is where you start.

THROUGHPUT

vLLM / SGLang

Serious concurrency, served behind an OpenAI-compatible private endpoint for real apps.

THE TRICK THAT MAKES BIG MODELS FIT

Quantization (Q4_K_M) roughly halves memory for minimal quality loss.

LAYER 03 · MODEL

The 2026 field splits in two

WHERE YOUR INFERENCE LIVES

Big MoE track

GLM, DeepSeek, Kimi, Qwen 397B. Frontier-ish, needs a Mac Ultra, multi-GPU, or rented H100s.

WHAT YOUR DEVS RUN

Consumer track

Qwen 3.6 27B, Gemma 4. Fits a single GPU, the current local default.

START HERE

Don't start with the biggest model on the leaderboard. Start with the best one that runs smoothly on your machine.
